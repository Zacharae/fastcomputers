Pediatric Care Study Sheet for NCLEX-RN

Growth and Development

Understanding normal growth and development is important in assessing and providing care to pediatric patients. The following are some key points to remember:

  • Infants typically double their birth weight by 6 months and triple their birth weight by 1 year of age.
  • Toddlers gain about 4-6 pounds per year and grow about 3 inches per year.
  • Preschoolers grow about 2-3 inches per year and gain about 4-5 pounds per year.
  • School-aged children grow about 2 inches per year and gain about 5-7 pounds per year.
  • Adolescents experience a growth spurt, with girls typically reaching their peak height at around age 12 and boys at around age 14.
  • Gross motor skills, fine motor skills, language development, and social skills are all important aspects of development that should be monitored.

Pediatric Assessment

Assessing pediatric patients requires special consideration, as they may not be able to fully communicate their symptoms or concerns. The following are some key points to remember:

  • Assessing growth and development is important in identifying potential developmental delays or problems.
  • Assessing pain and discomfort may require the use of age-appropriate pain scales or nonverbal cues.
  • Assessing vital signs may require the use of age-appropriate equipment or techniques, such as a tympanic thermometer or counting respirations while the child is asleep.
  • Assessing the child's level of hydration is important in identifying potential fluid and electrolyte imbalances.
  • Assessing the child's nutritional status is important in identifying potential nutritional deficiencies or excesses.

Pediatric Nursing Interventions

The following are some nursing interventions related to pediatric care:

  • Administer medications and treatments, as ordered.
  • Educate parents and caregivers on pediatric health and safety topics, such as safe sleep practices, car seat safety, and home safety.
  • Provide age-appropriate play activities and distractions during painful procedures.
  • Collaborate with other healthcare professionals, such as pediatricians, pediatric nurse practitioners, and child life specialists, to develop an appropriate plan of care.

Conclusion

Pediatric care requires specialized knowledge and skills, as well as an understanding of the unique needs and challenges of pediatric patients. Understanding normal growth and development, pediatric assessment, and related nursing interventions are all essential in providing high-quality care to pediatric patients.
