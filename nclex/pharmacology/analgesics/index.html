NCLEX Test Prep: Analgesics

Analgesics are drugs used to relieve pain. They work by blocking pain signals to the brain or reducing inflammation. As a nurse, it is important to understand the different types of analgesics and their uses, as well as potential side effects and nursing considerations.


Types of Analgesics

  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs): These drugs reduce inflammation and relieve pain. Examples include aspirin, ibuprofen, and naproxen.
  • Acetaminophen: This drug is used to relieve pain and reduce fever. It does not have anti-inflammatory properties. Brand names include Tylenol and Panadol.
  • Opioids: These drugs are powerful pain relievers that work by binding to opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord. Examples include morphine, oxycodone, and fentanyl.
  • Adjuvant analgesics: These drugs are used in combination with other analgesics to enhance their effectiveness. Examples include antidepressants, anticonvulsants, and local anesthetics.

Nursing Considerations

As a nurse, it is important to consider the following when administering analgesics:

  • Assess the patient's pain level before and after administering the medication.
  • Monitor for potential side effects, such as nausea, constipation, and respiratory depression.
  • Educate the patient on how to take the medication and potential side effects to watch for.
  • Monitor for potential drug interactions.
  • Assess the patient's risk for addiction and overdose.
