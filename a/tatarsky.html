Alex Tatarsky: The Multifaceted Clown and Actress

Alex Tatarsky is a clown, actress, and performance artist who has made a name for herself with her unique blend of physical comedy and political commentary. Born in Russia, Tatarsky studied at the Moscow Art Theatre School before moving to France to continue her training in physical theatre.

Her performances are characterized by their absurdity and their exploration of the human condition. Tatarsky's clowning is not just about making people laugh, but also about provoking thought and inspiring change. She uses her art to critique societal norms and to challenge her audience to see the world in a new way.

Tatarsky has performed all over the world, from Paris to New York to Tokyo. Her work has been recognized with numerous awards, including the Special Jury Prize at the 2019 Avignon Theatre Festival.

One of Tatarsky's most notable performances is her one-woman show, "Bardo," which explores the Tibetan concept of the afterlife. The show is a surreal journey through the stages of death and rebirth, and it showcases Tatarsky's incredible range as a performer. In addition to her solo work, Tatarsky has also collaborated with other artists and performers, including the acclaimed theatre company Les Chiens de Navarre.

Tatarsky's performances are not just entertaining, but also thought-provoking. She uses her art to challenge societal norms and to inspire her audience to think critically about the world around them. Her work is a testament to the power of performance to create change and to provoke thought.

Tatarsky's unique blend of physical comedy and political commentary has influenced a new generation of performers, and her legacy continues to inspire artists all over the world.
