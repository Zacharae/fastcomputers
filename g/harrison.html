George Harrison: A Brief Biography

Introduction

George Harrison was an English musician, singer, songwriter, and music and film producer who is best known as the lead guitarist of the Beatles. He was born on February 25, 1943, in Liverpool, England, and died on November 29, 2001, in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 58.

The Early Years

Harrison was the youngest of four children and grew up in a working-class family in Liverpool. He met Paul McCartney and John Lennon when they were all teenagers, and joined their band, which would later become the Beatles, in 1958. Harrison played lead guitar and contributed vocals, and was known for his distinctive sound and style.

The Beatles Years

As a member of the Beatles, Harrison was involved in writing and recording some of the band's most memorable songs, including "Something," "Here Comes the Sun," and "While My Guitar Gently Weeps." He also contributed to the band's experimental and spiritual direction, and introduced Indian music and culture to a Western audience.

Solo Career and Legacy

After the Beatles disbanded in 1970, Harrison embarked on a successful solo career, releasing albums such as "All Things Must Pass" and "Living in the Material World." He also collaborated with other musicians, including Eric Clapton and Bob Dylan, and founded the Traveling Wilburys supergroup. Harrison was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2004.

George Harrison's music and legacy continue to influence and inspire musicians and fans around the world, and he is remembered as one of the most talented and innovative guitarists and songwriters of his generation.
