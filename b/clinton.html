Bill Clinton

Early Life and Education

William Jefferson Clinton was born on August 19, 1946, in Hope, Arkansas. His father, William Jefferson Blythe Jr., died in a car accident before Clinton was born, and his mother, Virginia Cassidy Blythe, moved to New Orleans to study nursing. Clinton was raised by his maternal grandparents in Hope, Arkansas, before moving to Hot Springs, Arkansas, with his mother when he was 7 years old.

Clinton attended Georgetown University in Washington, D.C., where he graduated with a degree in international affairs in 1968. He then attended Oxford University in England as a Rhodes Scholar, earning a degree in philosophy, politics, and economics in 1970. He returned to the United States to attend Yale Law School, where he earned his law degree in 1973.

Early Career

After graduating from law school, Clinton returned to Arkansas and taught law at the University of Arkansas. He also became involved in politics, serving as Attorney General of Arkansas from 1977 to 1979 and as Governor of Arkansas from 1979 to 1981 and again from 1983 to 1992.

Political Career

In 1992, Clinton announced his candidacy for President of the United States. He campaigned on a platform of economic growth, healthcare reform, and deficit reduction, and he emphasized his experience as a governor and his ability to work across party lines. He won the Democratic nomination and faced off against incumbent President George H.W. Bush and independent candidate Ross Perot in the general election. Clinton won the election with 370 electoral votes to Bush's 168 and Perot's 0, becoming the 42nd President of the United States.

During his time in office, Clinton signed several significant pieces of legislation into law, including the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), and the Brady Bill, which mandated background checks for gun purchases. He also presided over a period of economic growth and budget surpluses.

Personal Life

Clinton is married to Hillary Rodham Clinton, who served as Secretary of State from 2009 to 2013 and was the Democratic nominee for President in 2016. The couple has one daughter, Chelsea. Clinton has been involved in various philanthropic and humanitarian causes since leaving office, including the Clinton Foundation and the Clinton Global Initiative.
