Dave Matthews Band

The Dave Matthews Band is an American rock band formed in Charlottesville, Virginia in 1991. The band consists of singer/guitarist Dave Matthews, bassist Stefan Lessard, drummer Carter Beauford, saxophonist Jeff Coffin, trumpeter Rashawn Ross, and guitarist Tim Reynolds.

History

Dave Matthews formed the band in 1991 after being introduced to Stefan Lessard and Carter Beauford. They began playing shows in the Charlottesville area and quickly developed a following. In 1993, they released their first album, "Remember Two Things," which was followed by their major label debut, "Under the Table and Dreaming," in 1994.

Over the years, the Dave Matthews Band has released numerous critically acclaimed albums and toured extensively. They are known for their unique blend of rock, jazz, and world music, as well as their improvisational live shows.

In addition to their studio albums, the Dave Matthews Band has also released numerous live albums and DVDs. Some of their most famous songs include:

  • "Ants Marching" (1994)

  • "Tripping Billies" (1996)

The Dave Matthews Band has won numerous awards throughout their career, including a Grammy Award for Best Rock Vocal Performance by a Duo or Group for their song "So Much to Say" in 1997. They are also known for their philanthropy, and have supported various environmental and social causes through their Bama Works Fund.
