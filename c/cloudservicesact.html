What is the Cloud Services Act?

The Cloud Services Act is a piece of legislation that governs the use of cloud computing services in the European Union.

What does the Cloud Services Act do?

The Cloud Services Act aims to create a common legal framework for cloud computing services across the EU. It sets out rules for data protection, security, and transparency for cloud service providers, and establishes a certification scheme for cloud services that meet certain standards.

Who is affected by the Cloud Services Act?

The Cloud Services Act applies to all cloud service providers that operate in the EU, regardless of where they are based. It also affects users of cloud services, as they must ensure that they are using a compliant cloud service provider.

When does the Cloud Services Act come into effect?

The Cloud Services Act was proposed by the European Commission in 2020 and is expected to be adopted by the EU in 2022. Once adopted, EU member states will have two years to implement the legislation into their national laws.
